What is Osteoporosis

A condition in which the bones become weak and fragile due to the reduction in the bone mass.

Post-menopausal women are affected the most. There is a reduction in bone mass and disruption in the architecture of bones, leading to decreased bone strength and increased risk of fracture.

Symptoms of Osteoporosis

An osteoporotic patient may exhibit the following signs and symptoms:

Bone Pain (Asthitoda)
Joint Pain (Sandhishoola)

Difficulty to walk (SandhiSaithilya)

Fractures (Angabhanga)

Fragile and soft bones (Asthisaushirya)

Peridontal disease (Dantabhanga)

Continuous bone pain (Asthibheda)

Spine bends forward (Vinamana)

Hair loss (Keshapatana)

Nakha Vikara & Pata (Brittle Nails)

Extreme fatigue (Srama)

Weakness (Balakshaya)

How Diabetes is Identified in the Human Body

HbA1c blood test

HbA1c forms when hemoglobin in the blood combines with glucose in blood. Levels of HbA1c gives an estimate of the average blood sugar over a week or month.

Fasting plasma glucose test

In this test, the patient’s blood sugar level is measured after the patient had been fasting for 8 hours. This test helps to detect diabetes or pre-diabetes.

Post prandial glucose test

In this test, blood sugar is measured after you have had your meal. This test helps in assessing how well is your body able to tolerate increase in glucose levels.
