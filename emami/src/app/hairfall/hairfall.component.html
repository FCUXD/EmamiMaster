What is Hairfall ?

Though losing hair is not a serious health issue, it has a significant impact on the aesthetics of an individual.

This leads to depression, loss of confidence and low self-esteem. No wonder hair-fall treatment is a billion-dollar industry today!

Symptoms of Hairfall

Gradual loss or fall of hair is the only sign associated with hair fall. The symptom of hair fall is exhibited by abnormal loss of hair which may be mild (loss of around 100 hair per day) or severe (accounts to loss of 100 to 200 hair per day).

Hairfall & the Doshas

According to Ayurveda, the imbalance in all three doshas contributes to the development and retention of obesity.

Kapha

The kaphadosha loses its normal consistency and becomes more vicious and sticky. As a result of these changes, Kapha along with Rakta Dosha blocks the opening of the pore, or hair follicles, in such a way that normal hair growth is inhibited.

PITA

Deranged pita dosha in the hair follicle act upon the root, and shaft of the hair till the hair tips cause hair fall. Deranged pitta also leads to a derangement of Rakta Dhatu (blood cells) and Kaphadosha.

VATA

Vata joins with body heat and burns the hair ground shortly, which eventually leads to baldness. Deranged vata dosha in the hair follicle act upon the root, and shaft of the hair till the hair tips cause hair fall.
