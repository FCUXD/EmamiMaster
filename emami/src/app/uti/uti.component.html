What is Urinary Tract Infection?

Any infection in the urinary tract, be it the kidneys, urinary bladder or the urethra, is called as urinary tract infection.

UTI is one of the most common bacterial infections that its estimated incidence is approximately 150,000 million per year globally.

How UTI is Identified in the Human Body

Urine Analysis

In this procedure the urine sample is sent to laboratory to examine for the presence of any bacteria, white blood cell, or red blood cell. The patient would be asked to wipe the genital area with an antiseptic pad and then collect midstream urine in a container.

Urine Culture

In this procedure, once the urine sample is collected, it is sent to laboratory for culture. This helps to find out the type of bacteria which is causing UTI and also helps the doctor to prescribe the effective medications

CT Scan / MRI of Urinary Tract

CT scan (Computerised Tomography) and MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) uses high frequency radio waves to develop images of the urinary tract. A contrast dye may be used to highlight the intended structures for examination. These images helps to examine the internal structure of the urinary tract.It helps in diagnosing various skin disorders such as infections or skin cancer.

Cystoscopy

Cystoscopy is done by using a long thin tube called as cystoscope. The cystoscope is inserted into the urethra and passed to the bladder. This is usually recommended for patients who have recurrent UTI. It helps in diagnosing various skin disorders such as infections or skin cancer.

Complications of Urinary Tract Infection

UTI if left untreated may lead to the following complications:

Permanent damage of the kidneys

Risk of delivering low birth weight babies or premature infants in pregnant women who has UTI

Recurrent UTI in women

Narrowing of urethra in men who has recurrent episodes of UTI

Sepsis may occur if the infection reaches the kidneys
