Erectile Dysfunction and Sexual Wellness

Understanding erectile dysfunction

Sexual wellness plays a crucial role in the life of every individual. Though discussing sex is considered a taboo, sexual health is as important as physical and mental health. In our modern fast-paced times, in addition to increasing prevalence of health problems such as diabetes or obesity, there is also an increasing incidence of sexual problems in both males and females.

Some of the common sexual problems in males are erectile dysfunction1 and ejaculation problems.2,3 Among females, it is the inability to reach orgasm, pain (dyspareunia), and failure of genital response due to poor lubrication. Varied treatment options are now available in both modern and Ayurvedic medicine, which are capable of completely curing sexual problems

Erectile dysfunction is one of the most common sexual problems among males. Though it is not a serious health issue, it does cause severe stress, lowers self-confidence, and gives rise to problems in marital life.

What is Erectile Dysfunction?

Erectile dysfunction refers to the failure to get erection and/or maintaining it till the end of ejaculation. It is also known as impotence.4

Erectile dysfunction may affect males of any age group, but is more common among those above the age of 40 years.4

Ayurvedic Approach to Erectile Dysfunction:

Ayurvedic literature describes erectile Dysfunction as Klaibya. This is seen only in males. The incident rate of this condition is higher in the present generation, owing to unhealthy eating habits, inactive lifestyle, and mental stress.2

Acharya Sushruta mentions Klaibya as aprharshamanandaabhavaha (lack of sexual arousal and happiness). In general, erectile dysfunction can be described as the persistent inability of the male to perform sexual activities due to a difficulty in getting an erection or a lack of ejaculation.

What causes erectile dysfunction?5,6

Some common triggers for sexual problems include:

1) Psychological factors (Manasikanidana):

  • • Psychological relationship problems
  • • Life stressors
  • • Anxiety or depression
  • • Low self-esteem
  • • Anxiety about sexual performance
  • • Excessive self-monitoring of arousal
  • • Feelings of guilt regarding sex
  • • Fear of pregnancy or sexually transmitted diseases (STDs)
  • • Lack of knowledge about sexuality or ‘normal’ sexual responses
  • • Previous significant negative sexual experience (such as rape or childhood sexual abuse issues)

2) Environmental factors:

  • • Environmental (fear of interruptions from children, parents)
  • • Physical discomfort
  • • Use of drugs or alcohol
  • • Side-effects of certain medication
  • • Pain or discomfort due to illness
  • • Feeling tired or ‘run down’
  • • Recent childbirth

3) Factors related to the partner

  • • Lack of sexual attraction (gender, physical characteristics)
  • • Evidence of disinterest
  • • Constant criticism
  • • Inconsideration and inability to cope with difficulties (esp. sexual)
  • • Lack of sexual experience and poor technique
  • • Performing sexual activities that are unappealing to the partner

Samprapti of Klaibya :6,7

There are four different types of klaibya according to Acharyas. They are explained as follows:

• DhvajabhangajaKlaibya:

Dhwaja in this context means penis and bhanga means change in the structure. Any change in the structure of the penis causes inflammatory changes and ulceration in the genital areas. This leads to pain and discomfort during intercourse. Gradually, the ulcer may penetrate the deeper tissues and destroy the blood vessels, muscles and nerves present in the male genitalia, leading to a permanent erectile dysfunction.

• BijopaghatajaKlaibya:

According to Chakrapani, factors like consuming dry or incompatible food, excessive food intake, excessive physical activity, sorrow or grief cause an aggravation of the vatadosha, and diminution of semen (Shukrakshaya). This may lead to erectile dysfunction.

• JarajaKlaibya:

This type of erectile dysfunction mainly occurs in old-age or due to consumption of anaphrodisiac foods (avrishyaahara). Such conditions cause a decrease in tissue fluids (rasa dhatu). In elderly males, the decrease in the level of testosterone (the main androgenic hormone) leads to a decrease in the production of sperms, which in turn decreases the force of erection and ejaculation.

• Shukrakshayaja Klaibya2:

According to Charaka, factors like regular consumption of hot and spicy food, psychological factors like anger or depression cause an increase in the vatadosha, which leads to a decrease in the tissue fluids. Subsequently there is progressive loss of all body tissue elements (dhatukshaya), including seminal fluid.

What are the symptoms to look out for (Rupa)5?

The symptoms or Rupa of erectile dysfunction are:

  • - Constriction of the penis (MlanaSisnata)
  • - Lack of ejaculation (Nirvirya)
  • - The absence of erection (Dhvajanuccharya)
  • - Inability to perform a sexual act (Suratasaktata)
  • - Profuse sweating (SvinnaGatrata)
  • - The absence of frothy formation in urine
  • - Swelling, pain and redness of penis
  • - Ring like hardness in the circumference of the penis
  • - Pain in the pelvis, testicles and groin

Can erectile dysfunction lead to other problems (Upadrava)?6

The following are the complications associated with erectile dysfunction:

  • - Low self-esteem
  • - Unhappy sexual life
  • - Strained relationships
  • - Psychological symptoms like anxiety and depression

Risk factors for Erectile Dysfunction9:

The following factors increase the susceptibility to erectile dysfunction:

  • • Old age
  • • Prolonged usage of medicines like steroids
  • • Psychological issues like depression or anger
  • • Obesity
  • • Lifestyle habits like smoking and drinking alcohol
  • • Medical conditions such as heart disease and diabetes
  • • Psychological conditions such as anxiety and stress

Diagnosis:8

.

Patient history: Patient information related to onset of symptoms, duration of erection, sexual drive, history of pelvic trauma, recent surgeries and other treatments around the pelvic area, medications, and comorbid conditions is gathered and clinically correlated.

Physical examination: The penis size and shape is determined, presence of conditions that may have caused secondary ED - such as thyroid conditions, sexual development - is assessed.

Lab tests:

The following parameters are evaluated:

  • 1. Serum testosterone levels: Low levels indicate that erectile dysfunction is hormonal in origin.
  • 2. Complete blood picture
  • 3. Thyroid profile and blood sugar levels
  • 4. Serum prostate antigen (PSA): To determine prostate diseases

Other tests: Some tests may be recommended to determine blood flow though the penis, such as penile Doppler ultrasound, penile angiography, dynamic infusion cavernosography/ cavernosometry

Vajikarana therapy4

In Sanskrit, Vaji means horse, which is a symbol of sexual potency. Thus, Vajikarana is producing a horse’s strength and stamina in the context of sexual activity.


Vajikarana is a type of ayurvedic treatment in which the potential of the man is increased by the use of ayurvedic medicines or therapy. This helps him to participate in sexual activities with greater strength and vigor (AstangaHridyaUttaraSthana).


Vajikaranaor Vrishyachikitsa is one of the eight specialities of Ashtanga Ayurveda. This method involves a strict adherence to a lifestyle that has been depicted in the Ayurvedic classics. The following are some of the therapies included in Vajikarana:


  • • Body cleansing (Shodhana) therapies

  • • Emesis (Vaman) therapies

  • • Purgation (Virechana) therapies

  • • Sweating (Swedana) therapies

The other components of therapy include- wearing fragrant garlands, body massage with medicated herbal oils, applying fragrant herbal pastes on the body, listening to music etc.

The above-mentioned steps of vajikarana help to revitalise the body elements (dhatus) and also restores the energy of an individual.

An Ayurvedic guide to Sexual wellness3:

Talking about sex has always been a taboo in Indian society. But since ancient days, Ayurvedic science has been providing excellent tips for improving the sexual health of both males and females.


Ayurveda considers sex and sexual intercourse not only as an activity that gives pleasure but also as one that enhances the emotional bond and mutual respect between both partners. The couple maintain equilibrium and achieve sexual satisfaction in an activity. In Ayurveda, Sex (Kama) is considered to be a valid and legitimate goal of human life.


One of the most vital aspects of sex in Ayurvedic literature is Shukra. Shukra refers to sperms, ovum, sex hormones and a matter-based potency in every cell. The presence of this Shukra in each cell is responsible for the regeneration of the cells. The food we consume undergoes several levels of metabolic transformation to form Shukra. It is present in our body since birth, but it acts as a major driving force in the body after puberty sets in.


The Shukra in our reproductive organs is the cause for procreation and that present in other parts of the body becomes responsible for the beauty and sexual attraction in both males and females. This effect of Shukra begins to decrease with the process of ageing.

According to Ayurveda, the following sexual cycles are helpful in healthy sexual management:

  • • Diurnal sexual cycle, practising sex in the nighttime is recommended than during day times.

  • • As the body’s shukra levels begin to deplete according to age, the ideal age to have good sexual activity is in the age between 18 years to 25 years.

  • • Avoid sex when women are in their periods or active menstrual cycle. This is to avoid the effect of aggravated vata dosha during this phase.

  • • Do not indulge in sex if you are too hungry or thirsty, and immediately after a meal.

Ayurveda also recommends the consumption of warm milk after coitus to replenish the lost vigor.

Ayurveda focuses on the all-round management of the condition rather than just the symptoms. Ayurvedic treatment, which includes medicines, diet and lifestyle changes, has been very effective in treating sexual disorders like erectile dysfunction without any side effects.
