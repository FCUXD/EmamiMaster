Piles or Hemorrhoids

Are you suffering from piles?1

One of the most common conditions that stops you from sitting comfortably on your bottom is piles or hemorrhoids. Although not very harmful to your health, it does cause a lot of distress. The chances of developing piles increases with increasing age.

The exact cause of the condition is not yet known. However, it is suggested that chronic constipation, chronic diarrhoea, pregnancy, improper diet, overweight or lifting heavy weights can all trigger the onset of symptomatic piles. There are a number of treatments available for piles. In some cases, home remedies and life style modifications are more than sufficient.

What is piles?1

Piles is a condition in which the veins of the anus and lower rectum region get inflamed and swollen. Piles occurs when the pressure on the blood vessels, in and around, the anus increases.

On the basis of location, piles is of two types:1

  • 1. Internal piles: Piles located above the opening of the anus
  • 2. External piles: Piles located outside the edge of the anus

On the basis of severity, piles is divided into four degrees:6

  • 1. First degree- bleed only, no prolapse
  • 2. Second degree- prolapse but reduce spontaneously
  • 3. Third degree- prolapse and has to be manually reduced
  • 4. Fourth degree- permanently prolapsed

Piles, as seen through Ayurveda 1

In Ayurveda, piles is known as Arsha, and is defined as:

‘Arivat pranan shrinoti hinastiiti arshah’

which means -Arsha is a condition that tortures a person’s vital body force like an enemy.10 Arsha is a disease condition in which a fleshy mass of different shape, size and colour is formed in the anus. In Ayurveda, piles has been categorized as a type of Ashta Mahagada, which refers to diseases that are caused due to hypofunction of digestive enzymes that may lead to constipation.

What causes piles? (Nidana/ Hetu)2

Piles occurs when the digestive fire (Agni) becomes abnormal or gets reduced, owing to improper diet and lifestyle. Disturbances in the digestive fire (jatharagni) impairs the balance of tridosha (Vata, Pitta and kapha), mainly Vata Dosha, and leads to the development of piles in the guda region (place for accommodation and elimination of the faeces).

Let us see some of the dietary and lifestyle factors that contribute to piles:

Diet factors- Ahara Nidana

  • 1. Having heavy food (guru), cold food (sheeta), kapha aggravating food (Abhishyandi), acidic (vidahi) and sweet food (madhur)
  • 2. Excess intake of black gram (masha/ Urad ), dry meat (ruksha mamsa), sugarcane juice (ikshu ras) oil cakes, germinated corns & pulses, freshly harvested crops & cereals (Navanna)

All these foods do not contain fibre and may cause constipation, which in turn leads to the protrusion of piles.

Lifestyle factors- Vihara Nidana2

  • 1.Sleeing during the day (Diwaswapna)
  • 2.Lack of exercise (Avayayam)
  • 3.Suppression of the natural urges to defecate (Vegavarodha)
  • 4.Excessive purgation (Ati virechana)

These lifestyle factors lead to the dosha imbalances which cause piles.

Classification of piles in Ayurveda

On the basis of effected dosha , it is classified into six types:3

  • Vataja- caused due to the actions of deranged vayu dosha
  • Pittaja– caused due to actions of deranged pitta dosha
  • Kaphaja-caused due to actions of deranged kapha dosha
  • Raktaja- caused due to vitated blood
  • Sannipataja- caused due to deranged vayu, pitta and kapha dosha all together
  • Sahaja- present since birth

On the basis of origin, piles is classified into two types:4,5

  • 1. Hereditary(Sahaja): This type of piles runs in families
  • 2. Acquired(Janmottarakalaja): This type of piles may occur due to improper diet and lifestyle

On the basis of the character of bleeding, piles can be classified into two types:5

  • 1. Bleeding piles (Stravi Arsha): Occurs due to impairment of Rakta(blood) , Vata and Pitta Dosha
  • 2. Non-bleeding piles (Shushka Arsha): Occurs due to impairment of Vata and Kapha Dosha

On the basis of the site, piles can be classified into two types:5

  • 1. Internal piles (Bahya)
  • 2. External piles (Abhyantara)

What piles does to your body (Samprapti)?4

The causes, or nidanas as mentioned above, lead to derangements in the digestive fire (jatharagni) which leads to tridosha imbalances, especially vata dosha. These affected doshas gets localised in the anal region (Gudavali) and blood vessels (Dhamani), and cause a disturbance in the digestive system (annavaha srotas dushti) which further affects the skin (Twak), muscles (Mansa) and fat (Meda) tissues. The derangements in these three dhatus (body tissues) leads to the formation of lumps which is known as piles.

The signs to look out for (Rupa)

The following are the common symptoms of piles:6

  • 1. Bright-red, painless bleeding
  • 2. Mucus discharge
  • 3. Prolapse
  • 4. Pain only on prolapse

Complications of piles (Upadrava)2

The following are the complications of piles:

  • 1. Anaemia (Pandu): It is rare, but excess blood loss from bleeding piles may lead to anemia.
  • 2. Thrombosed strangulated piles: Sometimes, a clot may form in the hemorrhoids which may further cut off the blood supply to the hemorrhoid. This causes intense pain.

Diagnosis of piles:

Piles is diagnosed by doing the anal examination (Guda Pariksha). There are three methods involved in clinical examination, i.e, Inspection (darshanam), Palpation (sparshanam) and Interrogation (prashna).

  • 1. Inspection (Darshanam): Visual inspection of the anorectal region helps in detecting the presence of an advanced case of I and II degree can only be diagnosed. The presence of internal haemorrhoids and thrombotic piles masses may also be diagnosed.
  • 2.Palpation (Sparshanam): Palpation helps in detecting the piles which are not evident on visual inspection.
  • 3.Interrogation (Prashna): It is important to get information from the person about the symptoms, type of pain and lifestyle habits. The pain described by the patient helps to determine the exact

cause of anorectal pain. E.g. throbbing pain in the anorectal region indicates abscess formation, whereas severe intermittent pain indicates a fissure. The rapid onset of severe pain confirms the thrombotic piles.

Some diagnostic tests for piles:2

  • 1.Proctoscopy
  • 2.Sigmoidoscopy
  • 3.Colonoscopy
  • 4.Barium enema

Cure piles with Ayurveda

Ayurveda helps the person to improve their overall health and lead a healthy life. Treatment for Arsha in Ayurveda has been classified into:

  • 1. Drug therapy (Bheshaja Karma)
  • 2. Surgical therapy (Shastra Karma)
  • 3. Chemical cauterization (Kshara Karma)
  • 4. Cauterization (Agni Karma)

Drug therapy (Bheshaja Karma):5

The mild form of piles can be cured by medicines alone, and does not need any surgical treatment. For treating piles, drug therapy can be administered in different ways, such as:

Oral medicines: The internal medicines are present in different forms:

  • 1. Rasaushadhi- Arsha-Kuthara, Nityodit Ras and Chakrakuthar Ras
  • 2. Choorna- Lavannothamandi Choorna, Nyagrodhradi Choorna, Kesar Choorna and Samangadi choorna
  • 3. Asava/Arists- Abhyarishta, Dantyarishta, Duralabharishta and Drakshasava
  • 4. Gud/Vatika- Suran Putpaka, Bahusala Gud and Kankayangutika

Topical medicines: Ayurvedic preparations are used for Panchakarma therapies, which help in reducing the pain of piles (Arsha)

  • 1. Oil massage with oils containing Chitraka, Yvaksharabilwa etc.
  • 2. Steam therapy (Swedan) with Rasoonpinda, Vijyapinda etc.
  • 3. Immersion therapy (Avagahan) with Arka, Kanji, Triphla, Agnimanth etc.
  • 4. Rectal enema using yastimadhu Taila, Chitrakadi Tailabasti, Kasissadi Tailabasti
  • 5. Fumigation therapy (Dhooma) along with Devdaru, Ghee, Pippali, Vidanga etc.
  • 6. Pastes (Lepa) for local application like Pippalyadipralepa, Haridrachurnapralepa, Snuhiksheer Mixed Hardidra Churnalepa

Surgical therapy (Shastra Karma):8

The surgical approach is used to treat piles. The most common surgical method used to treat piles is kshara sutra ligation (surgical ligation).

Kshara sutra ligation- It is a para-surgical method in which a herbal thread that is coated with Snuhi Latex (Euphortia Neriifolia), Apamarga Kshara (Achyranthes Aspera) and Haridra Churna (turmeric powder) is used. This medicated herbal thread is placed around internal and external piles, and within 5-7 days the piles mass gets removed. This is the most effective method to treat piles, with a cure rate of 97%.

What are the benefits of Kshara sutra?8

  • 1. It is a simple and safe treatment
  • 2. It has no complications
  • 3. Chances of recurrence are minimal
  • 4. Patients of all age can take this treatment
  • 5. Patients do not require any antibiotics
  • 6. It is day care surgery
  • 7. It is an affordable treatment
  • 8. This treatment can also be given to cardiac patient

Chemical cauterization (Kshara Karma):

It is a potential cauterization method to treat piles. Kshara, such as Apamarg kashara, Snuhi kashara, are caustic chemicals, which are alkaline in nature and obtained from ashes of medicinal plants. These agents are applied to the piles, which makes the walls of the bulging vein collapse, and contracts the pile mass. It is a flexible method that can be used in places, to treat piles that is difficult to be approached by other methods.

Cauterization (Agni Karma):

It is a method used when the condition cannot be cured by medicines or other methods. There are many different cauterization methods that are effective in treating piles. The treatment can be done by using laser surgery, cryosurgery, electrocautery or infrared radiation.

Prevention is better than cure

Pathya (Dos)8

According to Ayurveda, the basic preventive measure for a disease condition is to avoid the causative factors. The preventive measures for piles include:

  • 1. Prevention of constipation: Constipation can be prevented by using laxatives like Triphala Churna, Panchasakar Churna, Haritaki Churna and Abhayaarista
  • 2. Improve digestion (Deepan Pachan): Improve digestion by taking ayurvedic preparations, such as Chitrakadi Vati, Lavan Baskar and Agnitundi Vati
  • 3. Increased abdominal pressure: Avoid straining during defecation
  • 4. Dietary intake: Increase the intake of food with a high fibre content to improve the digestion
  • 5. Increase fluid intake: Drink enough fluids to improve the digestion and defecation functions of the body.
