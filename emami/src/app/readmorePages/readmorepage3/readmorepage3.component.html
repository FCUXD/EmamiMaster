An Ayurvedic take on Diabetes

Diabetes in a nutshell:

India is globally known for its contribution in Information Technology, Space Technology, Medical Advancements. But it also holds a place in being the diabetes capital of the world. According to WHO, India harbours the fastest growing population of patients with diabetes. Currently, India represents 49% of the world’s diabetics, with nearly 72.9 million suffering from diabetes. It is estimated that by 2045, about 134.3 million people will be afflicted with diabetes.

Urbanization, sedentary lifestyle, unhealthy food habits have all contributed to this staggering prevalence rate of diabetics in India. Thus, it is also termed as one of the major lifestyle diseases.

Diabetes, if not treated on time, may lead to life threatening complications such as coronary heart disease, kidney failure, neuropathies, diabetic coma and many more.

What is diabetes?

Diabetes is a disease condition in which the body is unable to effectively convert sugar (glucose) into energy. This leads to accumulation of sugar in the blood (high blood sugar).

Carbohydrate – Your body’s fuel

The food, that we eat, is broken down into carbohydrates, fats and proteins. Carbohydrate is converted into glucose, which serves as the fuel for all the metabolic processes in the body. This glucose is taken up by the body cells from the blood and is converted into energy.

Insulin is a hormone secreted by the pancreas. This hormone helps the body cells to take up glucose from the blood which is then converted into energy. The secretion of insulin depends on the energy requirements of the body.

Diabetes occurs when any of these following dysfunctions happen:

  • 1. The pancreas fails to secrete sufficient insulin
  • 2. The body cells are not responsive to insulin (insulin resistance)

  • Both these conditions lead to a decreased uptake of glucose by the cells, thereby causing hyperglycemia (or high blood sugar) and glycosuria (excess sugar in urine).

Diabetes in Ayurveda

Diabetes mellitus has been described in detail in the most well-known Ayurvedic texts, such as Charaka –Samhita, Sushruta-Samhita and Bhrigu-Samhita. Ayurveda describes diabetes under the name of ‘Prameha’. The Prameha is a syndrome that includes clinical conditions involved in obesity, prediabetes, diabetes mellitus, and metabolic syndrome.

What causes diabetes (Hetu)?

Diabetes can be classified into two types, based on the cause:

  • 1. . Sahaja(Genetic): This type of diabetes runs in families. It occurs at a very young age. Sahaja prameha and Jatah prameha are very similar to type 1 or insulin dependent diabetes mellitus. Decreased nutrition during important periods of growth in life- fetal life, infancy and childhood – have been known to contribute to type 1 diabetes.
  • 2. Apathyaja(acquired): This type of diabetes occurs due to factors such as obesity and unhealthy lifestyle. The characteristics of this type is similar to type 2 diabetes mellitus.

Causes of Apathyaja ( similar to Type-2):

  • 1. Sedentary lifestyle and lack of physical activity.
  • 2. Sleeping for long hours and daytime sleeping.
  • 3. Excessive consumption of puddings made of sugar and jiggery, flesh of aquatic animals, curds and milk.

Diabetes and the tridosha (three fundamental bodily bio-elements )

In Ayurveda, diabetes has been categorized as a type of Prameha which refer to a group of disorders that causes excessive urination and abnormal changes in the characteristics of urine. Prameha is considered to be a Tridoshaja Vyadhi (the disease condition that possesses the psycho physiological principles of all three doshas described in Ayurveda i.e Vata, Kapha and Pitta).

Based on the dosha which predominates, prameha is classified as Vataja, Pittaja and Kaphaja prameha. These three types are further divided into 20 subtypes:

  • • Kaphaja Prameha: This is further divided into 10 subtypes
  • • Pittaja Prameha: This is further divided into 6 subtypes
  • • Vataja Prameha: This is further divided into 4 subtypes

The Predominant Dosha and the diabetes type

According to Ayurveda, vataja prameha is similar to type 1 diabetes which is hereditary and has no cure. On the other hand, kaphaja prameha is similar to type 2 diabetes which is acquired and is curable.

What happens inside the body? (Samprapti)

Prameha is known to start with the aggravation of kapha that spreads all over the body and mixes with meda(fats), which has properties similar to kapha. This kapha-meda mixture passes through the urinary system and interferes with the process of urine formation and elimination. Such interference can also be brought about by deranged vata, pitta combination and other body toxins. This is believed to be the prime reason for excessive urination.

If not treated on time, aggravation of the doshas can lead to problems in the development of bone marrow, body tissues, nutritional elements (fat, proteins, and carbohydrates), and hormones (ojas). In Ayurveda, Madhumeha is described as a subtype of Vataja Prameha that can occur as the terminal stage of type 2 diabetes, or as type 1 diabetes beginning at early childhood. Defined as Jatah Pramehi Madhumehino in Charak Samhita, it is said to be an incurable form of Prameha.

Know the symptoms before it’s too late (Purvarupa)

Diabetes is a chronic disease. This condition is diagnosed late because the chief symptom, such as increased urination, is evident only during the later stages of the disease. According to Ayurveda, the following premonitory symptoms of prediabetes(Purvarupa) become evident due to the aggravation of the doshas:

  • 1. Excess thirst ( trishna)
  • 2. Excess sweating(Sweda)
  • 3. Sensation of sweetness in mouth( Mukhamadhurya)
  • 4. Weakness and fatigue( Shitilangata)
  • 5. Accumulation of waste on the teeth (Malin danta)
  • 6. Burning sensation in feet and hands (Hastapada Daha)
  • 7. Excessive growth of hair, and nails
  • 8. Dryness and coarseness of hair
  • 9. Bees and ants getting attracted to body and urine

Symptoms of diabetes (Rupa)

In addition to the above symptoms, the following signs are seen in patients with diabetes

  • 1. Excessive urination (Prabhoot mutrata)
  • 2. Turbid urine (Avilmutrata)

What happens when diabetes is left unchecked (Upadrava)

Based on the predominant dosha which has caused diabetes, the following complications are observed:

Complications seen in Kaphaja Prameha

  • • Indigestion (Avipaka)

  • • Nausea (Chardi)
  • • Excessive sleep (Atinidra)
  • • Cough (Kasa)
  • • Cold with running nose (Peenasa)

Complications seen in Pittaja Prameha

  • • Pain in bladder and urinary tract (Vasthimehanyotoda)
  • • Pain in testes (Mushkavatharanam)
  • • Fever (Jwara)
  • • Burning sensation (Daham)
  • • Acidity (Amlika)
  • • Giddiness (Moorcha)
  • • Diarrhea (Vitbhedanam)
  • •. Pain in chest (Hridayashoola)
  • • Insommia (Nidranasam)

Complications seen in Vathaja Prameha

  • • Tremor (Kambam)
  • • Gripping pain in chest region (Hridgraham )
  • • Pain (Soolam)
  • • Insomnia (Anidratha)
  • • Wasting (Sosha)
  • • Cough (Kasam)
  • • Difficulty to breath (Swasam)

Diagnosis of Diabetes

Some blood tests help in finding out if you have diabetes:

HbA1c blood test: HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin) forms when hemoglobin in the blood combines with glucose in blood. Levels of HbA1c gives an estimate of the average blood sugar over a week or month. Let us understand what your result means:

A1c Level Result
Less than 5.7% (39 mmol/mol) Normal
5.7% to 6.4% (39-46 mmol/mol) Prediabetes
6.5% (47 mmol/mol) or higher Diabetes
Fasting plasma glucose test:

In this test, the patient’s blood sugar level is measured after the patient had been fasting for 8 hours. This test helps to detect diabetes or pre-diabetes.

Post prandial glucose test:

In this test, blood sugar is measured after you have had your meal. This test helps in assessing how well is your body able to tolerate increase in glucose levels.

Oral glucose tolerance test:

In this test, the patient’s blood sugar is measured after 8 hours of fasting and then 2 hours after the patient drinks a glucose containing beverage.

Glucose Level 2 Hours After 75-gram Drink Indication
Less than 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L) Normal glucose tolerance
From 140 to 199 mg/dL (7.8 to 11.1 mmol/L) Prediabetes (impaired glucose tolerance)
Equal to or greater than 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) on more than one testing occasion Diabetes

Treating diabetes, the Ayurvedic way

Ayurveda has a holistic approach towards managing a disease. It aims at not only treating the disease but a patient as a whole. Management of diabetes in Ayurveda involves the following:

Herbal therapy:

Herbal therapy aims at normalizing the deranged doshas. The herbs for any illness are selected based on their properties- rasa(taste), guna(physiochemical properties), veerya(potency), vipaka(effect after digestion), prabhava(special actions), which have been found to manage the specific imbalance in the dosha.

Let us have a look of the herbs that are useful in treating the dosha imbalances

Herbs for Vataja prameha:
  • 1. Gokshura
  • 2. Asmantaka
  • 3. Arjuna
  • 4. Nimba
  • 5. Haridra
Herbs for Pittaja Prameha
  • 1. Usira

Herbs for Kaphaja Prameha

Ayurvedic Drug therapy:

Drugs prescribed for diabetes aim at regulating carbohydrate, and fat metabolism, therefore help in blood sugar control. Most common drug formulations are bitter (tikta) or astringent (Kashaya) in taste.

Keep diabetes under control, with a few simple lifestyle changes8,9

Ayurveda lays special emphasis on the importance of diet and lifestyle in maintaining health and managing diseases. Let us have a look at the various measures that can help you curb diabetes.

Dietary Measures (Ahara) :

Ayurveda considers diabetes to start mainly due to the aggravation of Kapha dosha. Thus, food and lifestyle habits that aggravate Kapha dosha should be eliminated from the diet. For this, food that is bitter, astringent, or pungent in taste should be taken whereas consumption of food that is sweet, sour or salty should be restricted.

The following foods have been recommended for diabetics:

• Cereals: Include barley, wheat and bajra

• Pulses: Chana, green gram(green moong), pigeon pea(tuvar dal)

• Vegetables: All bitter vegetables- bitter gourd(karela), fenugreek (methi), garlic(rason), cluster figs(udumbar)

• Fruits: Gooseberry (Amala), black plums (jamun), palm sugar, palm dates

• Seeds: Lotus and waterlily(Utpala) seeds

• Flesh: Deer meat, Rabbit meat

• Oil: Mustard oil, sesame oil

• Spices: All spices (except salt) such as peppers, mustard seeds, ginger

Keep diabetes in check with a few simple yoga postures

Certain yogic practices/ postures are known to strengthen muscular as well as pancreatic function. These include:

  • 1. Dhanurasana
  • 2. Ardhamatsyendrasana
  • 3. Halasana
  • 4. Vajrasana
  • 5. Naukasana
  • 6. Bhujangasana

Doing Surya Namaskara for 3-7 times/day has also been proven to be beneficial in improving health of patients with diabetes.

Breathe your diabetes problems away

Pranayama refers to a set of breathing exercises which aims to improve the pattern of breathing. It links both conscious pathways and unconscious pathways of breathing. Practicing pranayama helps in improving oxygen supply to the brain and other body systems. The pranayama practices that have been found to be beneficial for diabetes are:

  • Kapalabhati
  • Bhastrika
  • Om chanting
  • Anulom Vilom

Everyday exercises for diabetes

In addition to yoga and pranayama, doing certain light exercises as a part of your daily routine can be really helpful in reducing weight and controlling blood sugar levels. Performing physical activities helps in reducing kapha dosha. Give these fun exercises a try:

  • 1. Brisk walking
  • 2. Dancing and aerobics
  • 3. Swimming
  • 4. Cycling

As a general rule, all such moderate intensity exercises should be done for around 30 minutes a day, and 5 days a week. If a person is obese and diabetic, exercising around 40-50 minutes a day can be helpful in gradually reducing weight and improving blood sugar levels. While starting an exercise regime, initially start with a low intensity, and gradually increase the intensity of the exercises. Vigorous intensity exercises are not always recommended for lean diabetics.

Can irregular sleep affect diabetes?

Inadequate sleep leads to various hormonal imbalances. Poor sleeping for a long time can therefore cause a chronic imbalance in the hormonal levels and impair blood sugar control. Studies suggest that good sleep and meditation help to regain hormonal balance and effective blood sugar control.

Avoid these habits if you are diabetic (Aphathya)

  • 1. Avoid suppressing the urge to urinate
  • 2. Avoid smoking
  • 3. Avoid sleeping during daytime.
  • 4. Avoid sitting on cushiony surfaces a for long time.
  • 5. Avoid stress; practicing meditation and mindfulness could be of great help.
  • 6. Avoid the following foods (which aggravates kapha). Moreover, glycemic index and glycemic load of dietary item may also be taken into consideration.
    • Dairy foods- low amounts of low fat milk , ghee and low fat yogurt are permissible
    • Sugarcane juice
    • Meat of aquatic animals
    • Jaggery and sugary foods
    • Carbohydrate rich food (pishtanna)
    • Sour foods and beverages(kanjiika/ shukta)
    • Navanna(fresh cereals- recently picked rice and wheat)
