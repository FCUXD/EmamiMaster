Hair fall

Are you suffering from hair-loss?

Hair-loss is one of the most common problems all over the world. Though losing hair is not a serious health issue, it has a significant impact on the aesthetics of an individual. This leads to depression, loss of confidence and low self-esteem. No wonder hair-fall treatment is a billion-dollar industry today!

It is normal to lose around 50-100 hair strands a day. One should remember that hair fall is usually temporary, and lost hair grows back; it is a part of ageing. Excess hair-fall can be genetic, due to an illness, stress, iron deficiency, or as a side effect of any medication.

Hair enhances the beauty of an individual. Baldness is considered to be a curse. Even though hair fall is a very common problem, very few people receive treatment for it, considering the side effects of the available therapies. However, Ayurvedic science suggests the treatment for hair fall without any side effects – with an emphasis on a healthy diet and lifestyle modifications.

Can hair loss be treated with Ayurveda?

In Ayurveda, loss of hair is known as Khalitya. Ayurvedic treatment aims to address the root cause of hair fall.

Based on the causes, hair fall can be classified into four different types:

  • • Hereditary hair-loss
  • • Autoimmune hair loss
  • • Hair-loss due to underlying disease
  • • Hair loss as a result of chemotherapy

What causes hair fall? (Nidana)

Different types of hair fall have different causes, such as:

1) Diffuse scarring alopecia

  • a. Telogen effluvium (illness in previous 2– 3 months)
  • b. Post- partum
  • c. Androgenetic alopecia
  • d. Thyroid disease or iron deficiency anaemia
  • e. Certain drugs
  • f. Rare congenital causes

2) Scarring alopecia

  • a. Trauma: including radiotherapy
  • b. Tumours
  • c. Benign: naevus sebaceous
  • d. Malignant: Basal cell carcinoma (BCC), Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), metastatic deposits
  • e. Discoid lupus erythematosus (DLE)
  • f. Acne keloidalis

Pathophysiology (Samprapti)

Vata joins with body heat and burns the hair ground shortly, which eventually leads to baldness.

Hair follicles (romakupagata) contain bhrajaka pitta, a type of pitta, which promotes healthy hair. Khalitya is primarily a pitta dominant tridoshajanyavyadhi. Deranged pitta and vata dosha in the hair follicle act upon the root, and shaft of the hair till the hair tips cause hair fall.

Deranged pitta also leads to a derangement of Rakta Dhatu (blood cells) and Kaphadosha. The kaphadosha loses its normal consistency and becomes more vicious and sticky. As a result of these changes, Kapha along with Rakta Dosha blocks the opening of the pore, or hair follicles, in such a way that normal hair growth is inhibited.

Symptoms (Rupa)

Gradual loss or fall of hair is the only sign associated with hair fall. The symptom of hair fall is exhibited by abnormal loss of hair which may be mild (loss of around 100 hair per day) or severe (accounts to loss of 100 to 200 hair per day).

Alopecia areata (Indralupta) or hair loss in patches in the scalp is a common autoimmune disease that leads to hair loss on the scalp and in other body parts. It mainly occurs before the age of 30 years. The word “areata” indicates hair loss in patches. Alopecia areata usually starts with small, round bald patches on the scalp and progresses to total scalp (alopecia totalis) or even can progress to complete hair loss in the body (alopecia universalis).

Diagnosis

The diagnosis of hair fall is done through various steps known as TrividhaPareeksha (Tri-examination system) of the patient which includes:

1. Darshana (Inspection): In this process, the doctor would examine the extent of baldness, dandruff, and/ or dry hair roots. The doctor also examines if there is single or multiple patch of hair loss.

2. Sparshana (Palpation): In this process of examination, the doctor usually checks for dryness of the skin (saririkrukshata) to rule out VataDusti; teeth and bone health to rule out osteoporosis (asthikshaya) and AsthiPradhosaja (bone deformities); nail, eye brow and facial hair to rule out rakatajkrimi (infestation of parasites), dry, rough and yellow skin to examine chronic vitamin A toxicity, swelling and deformities in small joints to rule out rheumatoid arthritis, mouth ulcers, butterfly rashes to rule out SLE (systemic lupus erythematosus).

3. Prashna (Questioning): In this process, the doctor would ask questions to the patient related to

  • • Diet- like salt intake and other pitta aggravating food intake

  • • Number of hair that fall in a day (100 or more)

  • • Any scalp problems like dandruff

  • • Soap, shampoo, hair colors used by the patient

  • • If the patient is undergoing any particular treatment like chemotherapy, radiotherapy, anti-hypertensive drugs, anti-diabetic drugs, contraceptive pills, anti- epileptic drugs, long term use of steroid, etc.

  • • If the patient has any medical conditions like thyroid dysfunction, systemic lupus erythematosis, or rheumatoid arthritis

  • • If the patient is going through any sort of stress in the family, social, occupational or academic level

  • • Sleep pattern

Other tests that are recommended for the diagnosis of hair fall are listed below:

  • • Scalp biopsy: In this procedure, skin scrapes from the scalp or few hair will be plucked to examine the root of the hair for any infections that may lead to hair fall.

  • • Blood test: Blood test helps the doctor to find any underlying medical condition which is responsible for hair fall.

  • • Light microscopy: In this process, the doctor trims the hair at the bases and studies it under the microscope to check for any possible disorder in the shaft of the hair.

  • • Pull test: In this test procedure, the doctor may pull several dozens of hair together to check how many hair strands come out. This test helps to determine the severity of hair fall.

Stop hair fall, with the help of Ayurveda

Ayurvedic Therapy:

Panchakarma treatment which helps to restore hair growth, includes the following techniques:

  • • Shiroabhyanga: Massage of the scalp with warm oil to prevent dry scalp and enhance the lustrous nature of hair

  • • Shirolepa: In this process, a herbal paste is applied to the scalp. Paste is usually prepared by using herbs like triphala, amalaki, bhringaraj, neeli, etc.

  • • Nasya: In this process, medicated oil/liquid is administered through the nose, which enhances blood circulation.

  • • Shirodhara: Shirodhara is the process in Ayurvedic therapy that includes gently pouring of medicated liquids on the forehead. This technique of treatment helps to alleviate stress and strengthen the nervous system.

  • • Other external ointments (lepa) to prevent hair loss and graying of hair includes Murrayakoenigii (curry leaves), henna, black tea, coconut milk, fenugreek paste and yogurt.

Lifestyle Modifications:

Diet

A well-balanced diet providing adequate nutrition and proper digestion is very essential for healthy and lustrous hair. The following things should be included in the daily diet regime:

  • • Vegetables like kale, chard, collard, mustard greens, arugula, pumpkin

  • • Fresh juices of carrot, alfalfa, spinach, lettuce

  • • Sesame seeds (til) and fenugreek (methi)

  • • Whole grains

  • • Dairy products like milk, cheese, yogurt

  • • Fruits include all juicy fruits, coconut

  • • Nuts like Walnuts, raisins

  • • Spices like sesame seed (til), fenugreek (methi), cilantro, cumin, black pepper, turmeric, curry leaves

  • • Drink adequate amount of water regularly

Pathya

  • • Sleep adequately as it promotes rejuvenation of hair, prevents stress and increases energy levels.

  • • Use gentle and natural shampoos or hair products that are free from harmful chemicals.

  • • Wash hair with chlorine-filtered water.

  • • Practice dinacharya (Ayurvedic daily routine) that includes waking up in the morning before 6:00 a.m, eating the main meal during lunch and going to bed before 10:00 pm. This helps to balance the three fundamental doshas- vata, pitta and kapha.

  • • Brush your hair every night to stimulate the scalp and enhance the growth of healthy, lustrous hair. Use smooth long strokes from the scalp to the tip of the hair.

  • • Use wooden comb to avoid generation of static electricity.

Apathya

  • • Avoid heavy meals (Guru ahara).

  • • Avoid cold food (atisheetaahara).

  • • Avoid spicy food.

  • • Avoid excessive hot food (atiushna)

  • • Avoid sleeping at daytime (diva-svapna).

  • • Avoid incompatible foods (virudhaanna).

  • • Avoid frequent use of shampoo.

  • • Avoid using hair dryer

  • • Avoid suppressing natural urges.

  • • Avoid exposure to cold breeze during night outs.

Various diet and lifestyle modifications, along with Ayurvedic therapy like Panchakarma and Ayurvedic formulations, can effectively prevent and cure hair loss without any side effects. The effects of Ayurvedic therapy can be seen, not only in the disease symptoms but also in the general health of an individual.
