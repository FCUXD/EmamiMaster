What is diarrhoea?

diarrhoea is the common symptom of many underlying gastrointestinal problems.

People with diarrhoea have loose, watery stools along with symptoms such as stomach pain or cramps.

Diarrhoea may be acute or chronic. Acute diarrhoea lasts for less than three days and subsides on its own, whereas chronic diarrhoea persists for longer than four weeks.

How diarrhoea is Identified
in the Human Body

Stool test

A sample of stool is examined to check
for the presence of disease-causing
organisms and also to check for the
unusual constituents like toxins, blood
and mucus (ama, rakta and kapha).

Endoscopy

The doctor may recommend different
types of endoscopy such as colonoscopy or sigmoidoscopy,
to examine the bowels.

Complications in diarrhoea

Severe acute diarrhoea or prolonged diarrhoea leads to dehydration, manifests as the following symptoms

  • Thirst
  • Fever
  • Tastelessness
  • Pain in the rectum
  • Low blood pressure
